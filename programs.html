Our Programs

Holistic interventions for sustainable community transformation

Livelihood Food Security Trauma Healing Health & Wellness Education

Livelihood & Economic Empowerment

Fostering financial stability through sustainable income generation and community-managed savings.

Key Components:

  • Income Generating Activities (IGA): Vocational and entrepreneurial training in tailoring, baking, electronics repair, etc.
  • Village Savings & Loans Associations (VSLAs): Community-managed savings groups with 15-30 members.
  • Small Business Grants: Startup capital for micro-enterprises.
  • Market Linkages: Connecting producers to markets.
1,900 Beneficiaries
75 VSLAs Established

Food Security & Nutrition

Ensuring access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food through sustainable practices.

Key Components:

  • Emergency Food Aid: Distribution of food parcels during crises.
  • Urban/Rural Gardening: Training in vertical gardening, container farming.
  • Nutrition Education: Workshops on balanced diets and child feeding.
  • Small Livestock Rearing: Support for poultry, rabbit keeping.
1,875 Households Reached
500+ Gardens Established

Trauma Healing & Peace Building

Healing emotional wounds and building social cohesion in conflict-affected communities.

Key Components:

  • Trauma Healing Sessions: Group and individual counseling.
  • Peace Dialogue: Inter-community mediation and reconciliation.
  • Peace Advocates Training: Building local peace champions.
  • Community Resilience: Strengthening social support systems.
1,875 Individuals Healed
25 Communities Engaged

Health & Wellness

Promoting preventive healthcare and improving access to medical services.

Key Components:

  • Health Awareness Campaigns: Disease prevention education.
  • Mobile Clinics: Basic medical services in hard-to-reach areas.
  • Hygiene Training: Water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) programs.
  • Medical Referrals: Connecting critical cases to hospitals.
1,890 Patients Served
50+ Health Campaigns

Education & Empowerment

Breaking cycles of poverty through literacy and skills development.

Key Components:

  • Adult Literacy Classes: Reading, writing, and numeracy skills.
  • Computer Training: Basic digital skills for youth and adults.
  • Life Skills Education: Financial literacy, parenting skills.
  • Scholarship Support: Educational support for vulnerable children.
303 Adults Trained
85% Literacy Rate Achieved
